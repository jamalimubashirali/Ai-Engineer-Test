Campaign Brief: NextGen Eco-Sneaker Launch — "AeroLeaf" by Solstice Footwear

CLIENT: Solstice Footwear
PRODUCT: AeroLeaf — 100% biodegradable running shoe
CAMPAIGN TYPE: Product launch (awareness + pre-order conversion)
CAMPAIGN PERIOD: Q2 Planning → Q3 Launch (August 15th, 2025)
BUDGET: $250,000 (digital + out-of-home)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PRODUCT DESCRIPTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
AeroLeaf is Solstice Footwear's flagship product for 2025 — a performance running shoe
engineered entirely from next-generation earth materials. The midsole uses proprietary
algae foam (sourced from US freshwater bloom remediation programs), delivering 38% more
energy return than standard EVA foam. The upper is constructed from ocean-recovered nylon
filament, processed by our supply chain partner, Parley for the Oceans, from plastic
collected in coastal communities in Indonesia and the Philippines.

The outsole is natural rubber from certified sustainable forest tapping operations in
Costa Rica. AeroLeaf is certified carbon-neutral across its entire supply chain
(Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions) — verified by the Carbon Trust, UK.

Retail price: $110 (intentionally accessible to avoid positioning sustainability as
a luxury add-on). Available in 6 colorways. Sizing: US Men 6–14, Women 5–12.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Primary: Generate 8,000 pre-orders and 500,000 unique website visitors during the
6-week pre-launch window (July 1 – August 14). Secondary: Establish unaided brand
awareness of 15% among the core target demographic (eco-conscious urban athletes,
18–35) in markets: New York, Portland, Seattle, Los Angeles, and London.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TARGET AUDIENCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Primary Persona — "The Urban Athlete"
  Demographics: Ages 18–35, urban/metro locations (NYC, LA, Portland, Seattle, London),
  income $35K–$95K, college-educated, employed in tech, creative industries, or healthcare.

  Psychographics: Runs 3–5 times per week but also wears athletic gear socially.
  Considers sustainability when making purchases but has been burned by eco products
  that underperform. Follows fitness creators and sustainability advocates on
  Instagram and TikTok. Values aesthetics equally with performance. Responds to
  editorial-quality visual content, not stock photography or generic nature imagery.

  Key Tension: This audience WANTS to be sustainable but refuses to see it as
  a sacrifice. Any campaign that positions AeroLeaf as "the responsible choice"
  will fail. The correct framing: AeroLeaf is the superior performance choice that
  happens to cost the planet nothing.

Secondary Persona — "The Skeptical Competitive Runner"
  Demographics: Ages 25–45, runs 25+ miles per week, participates in organized races.
  Psychographics: Deeply skeptical of sustainability claims in footwear. Has tried
  "eco" shoes that compressed too quickly or lacked responsiveness. Responds
  exclusively to performance data, not values-based messaging. Must be reached
  through spec comparisons, athlete testimonials, and lab-verified performance claims.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TONE OF VOICE GUIDELINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The tone must be: Optimistic, bold, performance-forward, and future-oriented.

DO: Lead with performance claims. Use short, punchy sentences. Inspire — do not preach.
    Frame sustainability as an innovation story, not an environmental guilt story.
    Use specific numbers (38% energy return, 0.4 oz lighter, carbon-neutral since 2022).

DO NOT: Use the word "recycling" in any primary headline — use "reborn," "re-engineered,"
    or "next-gen" instead. Do not use generic green visual tropes (leaves, hands in
    soil, saturated greens). Do not make vague environmental claims — all claims must
    reference a verified source. Do not alienate performance-focused runners by leading
    with environmental messaging.

CONSTRAINTS AND RISK CONSIDERATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1. GREENWASHING RISK: All environmental claims must be supported by a named
   third-party certification. Any unverified claim will be immediately escalated
   to the Solstice legal team and may result in asset takedown.

2. COMPETITOR SENSITIVITIES: No direct competitor comparisons by name are approved
   without legal pre-clearance. Performance comparisons must use generic category
   benchmarks ("industry standard EVA foam") rather than named product names.

3. AUDIENCE ALIENATION RISK: Over-indexing on environmental messaging at the
   expense of performance claims risks alienating the competitive runner segment
   and reinforcing the perception that sustainable footwear is a compromise.

4. SUPPLY CHAIN TRANSPARENCY: Do not make claims about material sourcing that
   have not been confirmed in writing by the Solstice procurement team. Ocean
   plastic sourcing is verified; algae farm origins are confirmed for US supply only.

5. TIMELINE RISK: The June 15 concept deadline has zero flex — OOH production
   requires a minimum 6-week lead time for August 1 installation.
